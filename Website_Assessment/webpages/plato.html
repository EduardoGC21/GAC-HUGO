Plato

Born in 427 B.C. died in 347 B.C.
Plato was a famous philosopher of ancient Greece in Athens; he developed the now known way of doing philosophy “Dialectic,” he was the second of the great Athens Triad (Socrates-Plato-Aristotle). He wrote the Dialogues of Plato, where his wisdom and Socrates were written. He was the best student of Socrates and a teacher to Aristotle. He founded the school of Athens.
