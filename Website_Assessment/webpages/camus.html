Albert Camus

Born in 1913 A.D. died in 1960 A.D.
Albert Camus was a famous French philosopher. Renowned for his existentialist novels and his essays, like "The stranger" and "The myth of Sisyphus." In existentialism, he was recognized for his Absurdism view on life, where he states that life has no purpose.
