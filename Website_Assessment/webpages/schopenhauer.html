Arthur Schopenhauer

Born in 1788 A.D. and died in 1860 A.D.
Arthur Schopenhauer, also known as the pessimist philosopher, was a German philosopher. He wrote The World as Will and Representation. He developed his philosophical idealist system over Kant’s work. He wrote pessimistic views, despite some being true; he was vastly influential over Nietzsche’s texts.

"Where did Dante get the materials for his Inferno if not the real world?"

"How can it not be a crime if an eternal law punishes him with death?"

"We are like captive elephants who struggle furiously for a few days, but when they are convinced of the uselessness of their fury, they calmly accept the yoke and are tamed forever."

"The more lucid knowledge man possesses and the higher his intelligence, the more violent are his pains."
